# Budget Design & Justification Reference

Reference for designing realistic, defensible grant budgets.

## 1. Standard budget categories

### Personnel
- Salary + fringe benefits for each named person
- Effort percentages (typically 5-50% per person)
- Calendar months or person-months
- Trainee salaries (postdocs, students)

### Other Direct Costs
- Equipment (>$5K per item often classified separately)
- Supplies (consumables)
- Travel (per trip, justified)
- Publication costs
- Computing / cloud
- Subawards / subcontracts
- Consultants
- Animal costs (per diem)
- Subject incentives (clinical/human studies)

### Indirect (Facilities + Administrative) costs
- Calculated as % of direct costs
- Negotiated by institution with federal agencies
- Typical range: 30-70% of MTDC (modified total direct costs)
- Some categories excluded from base (equipment, subawards above threshold)

## 2. Personnel sizing

### PI effort
- Typically 10-30% on a single project
- Some funders require minimum (3 calendar months for some NIH mechanisms)
- Some funders cap maximum (95% across all support — must leave time for other duties)

### Co-investigator effort
- Typically 5-20% per co-I
- Match effort to actual contribution

### Postdoc effort
- Typically 50-100% on one project
- Salary follows NIH-set NRSA scale for biomedical fields
- Most foundation grants follow similar conventions

### Graduate student effort
- Typically 50% RA / 50% TA
- Stipend + tuition

### Research staff
- Project manager, coordinator, lab tech
- Justify based on workload (data collection, regulatory, etc.)

## 3. Salary discipline

### Salary cap (NIH 2026)
- ~$203,700 (Executive Level II)
- Effort over cap doesn't get reimbursed
- Institution makes up difference for high-salaried PIs

### Fringe benefits
- Institution-specific rate (15-35% typically)
- Includes retirement, health, FICA, etc.
- Multiply onto salary

### Cost of living adjustments
- Typically 3% per year for multi-year budgets

## 4. Equipment

### Threshold
- Often $5K per item (or $10K) defines "equipment" vs "supplies"
- Equipment is excluded from MTDC base in many indirect cost formulas

### Justification
- Why this specific item
- Why now (vs sharing institutional resource)
- Alternative options considered
- Useful life beyond grant

### Common errors
- "Need a microscope" — reviewer asks "you don't have one?"
- Buying equipment that should be at institutional core
- Major equipment in year 1 of a 5-year grant (sub-optimal use)

## 5. Travel

Realistic per-trip budget:

| Type | Domestic | International |
|------|----------|---------------|
| Conference | $1500-$2500 | $3000-$5000 |
| Collaborator visit | $1500-$3000 | $3500-$6000 |
| Data collection | varies by location | varies |

Per trip costs:
- Airfare
- Lodging (per diem or actual)
- Meals (per diem)
- Local transit
- Conference registration

### Mistakes
- Padding (10 trips/year when 4 are realistic)
- Under-budgeting (1 trip for a major conference)
- No justification (just a line item)

## 6. Subawards

When part of the work is done at another institution:

- That institution prepares its own sub-budget
- Their indirect rate applies to their portion
- Above $25K (NIH MTDC threshold) excluded from prime indirect calculation
- Letters of intent / commitment from subaward PI required

Common mistake: failing to coordinate subaward early; budgets and
letters arrive at deadline.

## 7. Supplies + consumables

Per project type:

- Wet lab: $20-200K/year
- Computational: cloud + GPUs ($5-50K/year)
- Clinical: subject incentives + materials
- Behavioral: smaller, but lit-review + analysis tools

### Justification
- Specific items (or categories)
- Approximate per-month / per-experiment cost
- Justified scope

## 8. Indirect costs (F&A)

### Federally negotiated rate
- Each institution negotiates with cognizant federal agency
- "On-campus" rate (typical for university research)
- "Off-campus" rate (lower, for work conducted away)
- "Other sponsored activities" (different from research; lower)

### MTDC base
- Modified Total Direct Costs
- Equipment > $5K excluded
- Each subaward over $25K excluded
- Tuition often excluded
- Patient care costs excluded

### Negotiating with foundations
- Many foundations cap indirect at 10-15%
- Some don't pay any indirect
- Institutional negotiation required for caps below institutional rate

## 9. Budget justification narrative

The justification document explains each line. Per person:

> [Name], PhD, [role], will commit [X] calendar months ([X]% effort) in
> Year 1. [Name] is [credentials] and will [specific responsibilities].
> Salary is calculated based on institutional salary of $[X], with [X]%
> fringe.

Per equipment / supplies:

> [Item] is required for [specific use]. The current institutional
> [alternative] is unavailable / inadequate because [reason]. [Item] will
> remain in use beyond the grant period for [purpose].

Per travel:

> Travel to [location] for [purpose] is budgeted at $[amount] based on
> [estimate basis]. [Number] trips per year are planned.

### Common errors
- Boilerplate justifications
- No specific quantities
- Hidden assumptions
- Generic "supplies as needed"

## 10. Budget realism — red flags

Reviewers look for:

- **PI effort below 10%** on a major grant — too thin to lead
- **PI effort above 50%** on a $100K grant — over-budgeted
- **Sum of percent effort across all grants > 100%** — over-committed
- **All-trainee budget** — who actually runs the project?
- **Equipment dominating year 1** — should be amortized
- **Travel pattern that doesn't match scope** — sketchy
- **Supplies inflation** — unjustified jumps year-over-year
- **Indirect rate doesn't match institution's negotiated rate**

## 11. Multi-year budget patterns

| Year | Effort pattern | Supplies | Equipment | Travel |
|------|----------------|----------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Ramp up | Setup | Major (if needed) | Conf + setup |
| 2 | Full | Steady | None usually | Steady |
| 3 | Full | Steady | None usually | Steady |
| 4 | Full | Steady | Some replacement | Conf + dissemination |
| 5 | Wind-down + writing | Lower | None | Conf + close-out |

Patterns that look like this read as realistic; flat budgets read as
artificial.

## 12. Budget review process

Before submission:

- Sponsored programs / grants office review (mandatory)
- Department chair / institutional approval
- Subaward institution approval (allow 4+ weeks)
- Compliance review (IRB, IACUC, biosafety, export control)
- Conflict-of-interest disclosure

Standard pre-submission lead time: 5-10 business days for institutional review.

## 13. Common pitfalls

- **Budget submitted without sponsored-programs review.** Will bounce back.
- **PI effort doesn't add to a credible total.** Looks under-engaged.
- **Sum of all-source PI effort > 100%.** Triggers institutional flag.
- **Equipment dominating.** Reviewer wonders about institutional support.
- **Vague supplies.** "Supplies as needed: $50K." Won't pass.
- **No travel justification.** Looks padded.
- **Indirect cost rate wrong.** Bounces back from grants office.
- **Subaward documentation missing.** Holds up institutional approval.
- **Foundation cap on indirect missed.** Institutional shortfall.
- **No cost-share when required.** Some grants require matching.
