# ICP — Dimensions Deep Reference

## 1. Firmographics

Standard observable attributes of a company:

### Industry / vertical
- North American Industry Classification System (NAICS)
- Tech-vertical taxonomies (SaaS, fintech, healthtech, etc.)
- Be specific: "SaaS" is broad. "B2B SaaS" narrower. "B2B SaaS in HR tech" sharper.

### Size
- Employee count (200-2000 EE)
- Revenue ($10M-$100M ARR)
- Funding stage (Series B-D)
- Customer count (mid-market vs enterprise)

Don't conflate these. A 50-person AI startup with $50M funding is
different from a 500-person services company with same revenue.

### Geography
- Country / region
- Specific cities (for in-person sales)
- Regulatory jurisdiction
- Language / culture cluster

### Age + maturity
- Years in operation
- Funding stage (signals maturity + capital)
- Public vs private
- VC-backed vs bootstrapped vs PE-owned

### Growth stage
- Pre-PMF / growth / mature / decline
- Headcount growth rate
- Revenue growth rate

## 2. Tech-stack signals

The tools they use reveal maturity, openness, and budget.

### Categories to identify
- CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive — none?)
- HRIS (Workday, BambooHR — Excel?)
- Comms (Slack, Teams — email?)
- Data warehouse (Snowflake, BigQuery — none?)
- BI tools (Looker, Tableau, Mode)
- Cloud provider (AWS, GCP, Azure)
- Languages (Python, Java, Go — signals tech sophistication)

### Tools to discover stack
- BuiltWith / Wappalyzer
- Crunchbase tech stack
- G2 user lists
- LinkedIn employee skills
- GitHub orgs
- Job descriptions (what stack they hire for)

### Stack as ICP signal
- Have a CRM = structured sales motion
- Have a data warehouse = analytics-mature
- Have an established HRIS = HR function beyond founder
- Have specific vendor X = open to X's category

## 3. Buyer persona

The human(s) who decide / influence the buy.

### Per persona, document
- **Title:** their job title (and variants)
- **Level:** IC / Manager / Director / VP / C-Level
- **Team size:** how many they manage
- **Tenure:** how long in this role
- **Background:** what they did before
- **Motivations:** career, problems, recognition
- **Anxieties:** what they fear (failure, irrelevance)
- **Day in life:** what they do hour-by-hour
- **Information sources:** podcasts, newsletters, communities
- **Quotes from interviews:** verbatim, anonymized

### Persona types
- **Economic buyer:** signs the check
- **Technical buyer:** evaluates fit + integration
- **End user:** uses daily
- **Champion:** internal advocate
- **Decision influencer:** weighs in

A single deal usually has 5-12 personas in buying committee.

## 4. Jobs-to-be-done (JTBD)

What the customer is trying to accomplish.

### JTBD framing (Clayton Christensen)
"When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]."

### Example
- Situation: Monthly HR board reports
- Motivation: Reduce time + errors + look professional
- Outcome: Be a strategic partner to CFO/CEO, not just an admin

### JTBD types
- **Functional JTBD:** the task (submit expense report)
- **Emotional JTBD:** how they want to feel (competent, in-control)
- **Social JTBD:** how they want to appear (strategic, not bureaucratic)

A complete ICP touches all three.

### JTBD vs needs
- "Need a CRM" = solution-thinking
- "Want to keep customer info accessible to whole team" = JTBD
- "Want to look like a real sales operation to my CEO" = social JTBD

Sales pitches that map to JTBD resonate; pitches mapping to needs feel transactional.

## 5. Existing alternatives

How customers solve the JTBD today (before you).

### Always exists
"Doing nothing" is an alternative. "Using a competitor" is too.

### Categories
- Direct competitor (same category)
- Substitute (different category, same JTBD)
- DIY (built in-house)
- Manual workaround (spreadsheet, agency)
- Doing nothing / suffering with the pain

### Why this matters
- Your value prop must beat the alternative (not just be present)
- Switching cost from alternative = your friction
- Alternative's price = your price ceiling (often)

## 6. Trigger events (why now)

Most-overlooked dimension; biggest predictor of buying activity.

### Strong triggers (60-180 day buying windows)
- New executive hired (60-day window)
- Funding round closed (90-day window)
- M&A announced (180-day window)
- New regulation enacted (varies)
- Annual planning cycle (Q4-Q1)
- Major incident / breach (immediate)
- Vendor consolidation push (30-day window)
- Headcount change crossing a threshold (e.g., 50 → 500 EE)
- Reorg / department restructure (90-day window)

### Weak / no triggers
- "They have the budget"
- "They mentioned it once"
- "Their competitor uses us"
- "They might want it"

### Trigger sources
- News / press releases
- LinkedIn (job changes, posts)
- SEC filings
- Crunchbase
- BuiltWith (tech-stack changes)
- Their job postings (what they're hiring for)
- Conference / event attendance
- Funding databases

### Tools
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- Apollo / ZoomInfo (trigger filters)
- Crunchbase Pro
- Diffbot / news aggregators

## 7. Budget authority

Who controls the relevant spend.

### Per persona, document
- **Direct budget owner:** can approve up to $X
- **Authorization threshold:** above $X needs additional approval
- **Procurement involvement:** required above $Y
- **Board involvement:** required above $Z

### Common authority levels
- Individual / IC: < $1K (corporate card)
- Manager: $1K - $25K
- Director: $25K - $100K
- VP: $100K - $500K
- C-Level: $500K - $2M
- Board: > $2M (often)

Numbers vary by company size + culture.

### Multi-stakeholder authority
Above ~$50K, multiple people often weigh in:
- Department head (budget owner)
- Finance (approver)
- Procurement (process)
- Legal (contracts)
- Security (review)
- IT (integration)
- Sometimes: end user community

## 8. Reachability

Can you actually find + contact this ICP?

### Reachability tests
- **Paid acquisition:** is the audience addressable on LinkedIn / Google / etc.? Volume? CPC?
- **Outbound:** can your BDR build a target list of 500+ via Apollo / LinkedIn?
- **Events:** which 2-3 events does this ICP attend?
- **Content / SEO:** what do they search for? Search volume?
- **Communities:** which Slack / Discord / subreddit / newsletter?
- **Partners:** which adjacent vendors have your ICP as their customer?

### Unreachable ICPs
Some ICPs are economically unaddressable:
- Audience too small for paid acquisition
- No events; no community
- Outbound list under 200
- No content angle

If you can't find them, you can't sell to them — even if they'd love the product.

## Strong vs weak ICP examples

### Weak
> "Mid-sized companies that want to be data-driven"

Vague firmographics. No JTBD. No trigger. Unaddressable.

### Strong
> "US-headquartered B2B SaaS, 200-2000 EE, Series B-D, that recently hired
> a VP of Revenue Operations who's tasked with 'getting visibility into
> sales pipeline.' Buyer is the VP RevOps (often new in role, 0-90 days),
> who is replacing a manual Excel + Salesforce-export workflow. Decision
> usually $25-100K, approved by VP RevOps with CRO sign-off. Triggered
> by hiring of VP RevOps or board pressure on forecast accuracy.
> Reachable via: LinkedIn Sales Nav search for 'VP RevOps' in target
> firmographics (~1200 results); SaaStr Annual (3000+ attendees); Pavilion
> RevOps community (~5000 members); RevGenius newsletter."

Specific. Testable. Targetable. Triggered.

## ICP refresh signals

- Top 20 closed customers don't all match your stated ICP → refine
- Sales win rate higher in segment X than your stated ICP → expand
- High-churn customers cluster in your stated ICP → tighten
- New product capability opens new ICP → add as a segment
- Market shift (regulation, recession) → re-evaluate
