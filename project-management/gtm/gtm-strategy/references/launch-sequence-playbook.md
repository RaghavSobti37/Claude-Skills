# Launch Sequence Playbook

T-90 to T+90, role by role.

## T-90 — Lock the foundations

### PM
- ICP locked + documented
- Positioning v1
- Pricing + packaging finalized
- Feature scope locked for launch
- Roadmap post-launch sketched

### Marketing
- Launch theme / narrative
- Hero message + 3 supporting messages
- Channel strategy (which to invest)
- Content plan (blog, video, social)
- Analyst targets identified

### Sales
- Sales playbook outline
- Lighthouse customer targets
- Sales kickoff date booked
- Compensation plan updated for new offering

### CS
- Customer success playbook outline
- Onboarding flow
- Support training plan

### CEO
- Investor / board narrative
- Executive sponsorship for launch
- Major partnerships engaged

### Finance
- Launch budget approved
- Success metrics + targets
- Forecast model

## T-60 — Build the assets

### PM
- Demo scripts
- Customer feedback from beta
- Product-market fit signal review
- Documentation in user-facing channels

### Marketing
- Landing page live (gated)
- Press kit drafted
- Analyst briefings scheduled
- Influencer outreach started
- Webinar (if planned) scheduled
- Social calendar drafted

### Sales
- Sales deck v1
- Battle cards (vs alternatives)
- ROI calculator
- 1-pager + case-study skeletons
- Lighthouse customers signed up

### CS
- Onboarding sequence
- Customer training materials
- Health-check rubric

### Press / analysts
- Embargoed briefings
- Spokesperson prepped

## T-30 — Train + rehearse

### Internal
- Sales kickoff (full team)
- All-hands launch preview
- CS + support training
- Engineering on-call ready for spike

### External
- Lighthouse customers public-OK confirmed
- Analyst pre-briefings complete
- Press embargoes signed
- Pre-launch email to existing customers (heads-up)

## T-7 — Final prep

### All teams
- Final comms reviewed (legal sign-off)
- Launch dashboards live
- War-room set up
- Crisis comms drafted (in case of bad press)
- Backup plans (server capacity, etc.)

### Final list
- [ ] Press release approved
- [ ] Blog post scheduled
- [ ] Email queued
- [ ] Social posts queued
- [ ] Lighthouse customer quotes confirmed
- [ ] Sales team ready to take inquiries
- [ ] Support team ready for support tickets
- [ ] On-call eng ready for spike
- [ ] Analyst day-of contact info ready

## T-0 — Launch day

### Hour 0
- Press release goes live
- Blog post live
- Social posts go live
- Email goes out
- Sales team ready for inbound
- Support team ready

### Hours 1-8
- Monitor real-time:
  - Site traffic
  - Sales inquiries
  - Support tickets
  - Social mentions
  - Press pickup
- Quick wins: re-share early positive press
- Address issues immediately

### End of day
- Daily standup with all teams
- Top issues triaged
- Next-day priorities set

## T+1 to T+7 — Triage + iterate

### Track
- Channel performance: which delivered?
- Customer questions: patterns?
- Sales early-stage: where are deals stalling?
- Demo conversion: requested vs converted?
- Lighthouse customer happiness: maintain trust

### Act
- Fix messaging that confuses
- Add FAQs to address common questions
- Adjust paid channel investment based on signal
- Push early wins (case studies, testimonials)
- Brief sales on common objections + responses

## T+30 — First learning wave

### Review
- Pipeline generated vs target
- Demo → opportunity → close rates (early signal)
- Customer satisfaction (new customers)
- Channel attribution
- Competitive response

### Adjust
- Messaging refinement based on what sticks
- Channel reallocation (double down what works; cut what doesn't)
- Sales playbook v2
- Pricing tweaks (rare; only if needed)

## T+90 — GTM v2

### Review
- Beachhead success metrics
- ICP refinement based on actual customers
- Motion economics (CAC payback, LTV/CAC)
- Channel mix optimization
- Sales rep performance + ramp curves

### Plan
- Next beachhead segment
- Expansion plan (geography / vertical / persona)
- GTM v2 with learnings incorporated
- Roadmap updates based on customer feedback

## Common launch failures

### "Launched into silence"
**Symptom:** Press release goes out; nobody notices.

**Cause:** No press relationships; no analyst briefings; no narrative.

**Fix:** PR / analyst engagement begins T-90 minimum.

### "Sales team unprepared"
**Symptom:** Inquiries come; sales doesn't know how to handle.

**Cause:** Sales kickoff was day-of; not weeks before.

**Fix:** Sales trained T-30; playbook + battle cards in hand.

### "Spike then silence"
**Symptom:** Launch day is huge; week 2 is nothing.

**Cause:** Launch was a moment, not a campaign.

**Fix:** Campaign continues T+30 with content, customer stories, integration partner activity.

### "Customer pile-up"
**Symptom:** Tons of signups; CS can't onboard them all.

**Cause:** Demand exceeded CS capacity.

**Fix:** Either gate signups or scale CS capacity pre-launch.

### "Press / analyst silence"
**Symptom:** No coverage from major outlets / analysts.

**Cause:** Embargoes were too tight; story didn't differentiate; relationships not built.

**Fix:** Start press / analyst relationships months in advance.

### "Launched the wrong message"
**Symptom:** Messaging doesn't resonate; bounce rate high; demos low.

**Cause:** Messaging wasn't customer-tested.

**Fix:** A/B test messaging T-30 with beta customers + paid traffic.

## Cadence per audience after launch

| Audience | Cadence |
|----------|---------|
| Customers (existing) | Email at T-7, T-0, T+30 |
| Prospects (in pipeline) | AE-led; weekly cadence |
| Press | T-30 embargoed; T-0 release; T+30 follow-ups |
| Analysts | T-30 pre-brief; T-0 brief; T+90 update |
| Partners | T-30 enablement; T-0 announce; T+30 reflection |
| Investors | T-7 heads-up; T+30 update; T+90 results |
| Internal | T-30 kickoff; T-0 launch; T+30 retro |

## Launch budget allocation (rough)

For a meaningful product launch:

- PR / analyst: 15-25%
- Paid acquisition (initial spike): 30-40%
- Content (case studies, video, blog): 15-20%
- Events (if any): 10-15%
- Sales enablement: 5-10%
- Tooling (dashboards, automation): 5-10%

Total often $200K-$2M depending on company stage + launch importance.
