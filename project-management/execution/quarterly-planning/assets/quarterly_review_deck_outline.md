# Quarterly Review Deck Outline

A deck for presenting quarterly results upward (leadership, all-hands, or board). The deck is *not* the close retro -- it is the communication of results to a broader audience.

**Target length:** 10-15 slides
**Target duration:** 20-30 minutes presentation + 10-15 minutes Q&A
**Audience:** Executive team, partner teams, board, or all-hands (adjust depth accordingly)

---

## Slide 1: Title

**Title:** Q[X] [Year] Review -- [Team Name]
**Date:** [...]
**Presenter:** [PM name]

---

## Slide 2: Quarter at a Glance

A one-slide summary:

- **Objective(s):** [1-3 short statements]
- **Average OKR Score:** [X.X] (target zone 0.6-0.7)
- **Headline outcome:** [One sentence on the biggest win or movement]
- **Headline learning:** [One sentence on the most important learning]

**Visual:** A scorecard showing each KR with target vs. actual and a color (green/yellow/red).

---

Quick celebration of team members and partners who made the quarter possible.

**Norm:** Specific shoutouts beat generic team thanks. "Sarah's work on the Xero integration unblocked KR2" beats "thanks team."

---

## Presentation Tips

- **Time-box the opening.** Slide 2 (Quarter at a Glance) should land in 90 seconds. Skip if you can.
- **Lead with the score.** Don't bury the average OKR score; put it on slide 2.
- **Anchor every learning to evidence.** "We learned X" should reference a specific signal, customer, or data point.
- **Make asks concrete.** Specific people, specific commitments, specific dates.
- **Save 10-15 min for Q&A.** Strong presenters present 20-25 min and leave time for discussion.
- **Pre-share the deck.** Send the deck 24 hours in advance so the audience reads it before the meeting; use the meeting for Q&A and decisions.
