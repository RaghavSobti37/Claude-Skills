# Fit Validation Checklist

Three levels of fit to validate, in order. Each level has evidence requirements; do not claim a level without the evidence.

---

## Level 1: Problem-Solution Fit

**Definition:** We have designed value-creating products and services that customers want.

**Evidence requirements (all must be met):**

- [ ] **Customer Profile validated.** Jobs, pains, and gains populated from >=5 customer interviews per segment, with verbatim quotes attached.
- [ ] **Top 3 jobs identified.** Importance + frequency scoring complete; team aligned.
- [ ] **Top 3 pains identified.** Severity x frequency scoring complete; current workarounds documented.
- [ ] **Top 3 gains identified.** Including at least 1 Unexpected (delighter) gain.
- [ ] **Value Map mirrors top items.** Every top-3 job, pain, and gain has a corresponding pain reliever or gain creator.
- [ ] **Concept tests passed.** At least 5 customers reviewed the value proposition statement and confirmed it resonates with their actual problem (not "sounds interesting" -- "this is what I'm dealing with").
- [ ] **Willingness-to-pay signal.** At least 3 prospects have signaled intent to pay (LOI, prepayment, paid pilot) -- not "interested in seeing more".
- [ ] **No forced-fit mapping.** Items with no genuine pain reliever or gain creator are explicitly listed as uncovered.

**Conclusion:**

- [ ] Problem-Solution Fit ACHIEVED -- proceed to validate Product-Market Fit
- [ ] Problem-Solution Fit PARTIAL -- list specific gaps and a plan to close them
- [ ] Problem-Solution Fit NOT YET -- return to discovery (`discovery/customer-interview-script/`)

## Level 2: Product-Market Fit

**Definition:** We have evidence that customers want our products and services and will pay for them.

**Evidence requirements (most must be met; weight depends on stage):**

- [ ] **Sean Ellis test.** Survey existing users with: "How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?" -- at least 40% answer "Very disappointed".
- [ ] **Retention curve flattens.** Cohort retention does not approach zero; segment-appropriate plateau reached (e.g., B2B SaaS: >70% retention at 90 days).
- [ ] **Net Revenue Retention.** > 100% on a trailing 12-month basis (B2B SaaS).
- [ ] **Organic growth signal.** Net new MRR from referrals/word-of-mouth > 0 when paid acquisition is zeroed.
- [ ] **Willingness-to-pay confirmed.** Customers are paying at or above the target ACV/ARPU; price increases tested and accepted.
- [ ] **Customer NPS.** > 30 (B2B) or > 50 (B2C consumer).
- [ ] **Expansion revenue.** Customers are upgrading tiers, adding seats, or buying add-ons within 12 months.
- [ ] **Win rate.** > 25% on qualified opportunities.

**Conclusion:**

- [ ] Product-Market Fit ACHIEVED -- proceed to validate Business Model Fit; consider scaling
- [ ] Product-Market Fit PARTIAL -- specific signals missing; targeted experiments to close
- [ ] Product-Market Fit NOT YET -- return to problem-solution validation; pivot may be needed

## Level 3: Business Model Fit

**Definition:** We have a scalable, profitable business model.

**Evidence requirements (all must be met for sustainable scale):**

- [ ] **LTV / CAC.** > 3 on a fully-loaded basis (CAC includes sales + marketing + onboarding).
- [ ] **CAC Payback Period.** < 18 months (B2B SaaS); < 12 months (PLG); < 3 months (B2C subscription).
- [ ] **Gross Margin.** > 70% (B2B SaaS); appropriate to business model.
- [ ] **Channel economics scale.** Cost per acquired customer does not balloon as the channel scales 2-3x.
- [ ] **Operating model sustainable.** Burn multiple < 2 (Series A); < 1 (Series B+); positive contribution margin per customer.
- [ ] **Unit economics by segment.** LTV/CAC holds across at least 2 distinct segments (no single-segment dependency).
- [ ] **Pricing tested at multiple price points.** Customers retained after a price increase or willing to upgrade tiers.

**Conclusion:**

- [ ] Business Model Fit ACHIEVED -- scale with confidence
- [ ] Business Model Fit PARTIAL -- identify economic gaps and address before scaling spend
- [ ] Business Model Fit NOT YET -- defer scaling; reduce burn; revisit pricing and unit economics

## Anti-Pattern Checklist

Things that look like fit but are not. If any of these are true, downgrade the fit claim by one level.

- [ ] We are claiming PMF based on interview enthusiasm rather than behavior.
- [ ] We have growth but it is bought, not retained (LTV < CAC).
- [ ] We have retention but only in one segment that does not scale.
- [ ] We have revenue but margins are unworkable.
- [ ] We have a strong Value Map but the Customer Profile is written in team language, not customer language.
- [ ] We have an "all customers" canvas instead of segment-specific canvases.
- [ ] Our roadmap targets fix items that are NOT in the top 3 pains or gains.
- [ ] We assumed problem-solution fit transferred to a new segment without re-validating.

---

## Quarterly Review Cadence

Fit is not a one-time achievement. Schedule:

- **Monthly:** Behavioral signals (retention, NRR, NPS)
- **Quarterly:** Full canvas review, including Customer Profile drift and uncovered items
- **Annually:** Business model fit review, including unit economics by segment

**Triggers for early review:**

- Retention drop > 5pp month-over-month
- Major competitor move
- Segment entry (new market, new role)
- Pricing change
- Product line expansion or sunset
