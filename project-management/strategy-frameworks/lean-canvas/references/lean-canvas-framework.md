# Lean Canvas — Framework Reference

Ash Maurya's adaptation of BMC for startups.

## 1. Why a different canvas?

BMC works for established businesses. Startups face different uncertainty:
- Problem is uncertain (does anyone have it?)
- Solution is uncertain (does it actually solve the problem?)
- Channel is uncertain (can we reach customers economically?)
- Revenue model is uncertain (will they pay?)

Lean Canvas surfaces these uncertainties. BMC papers over them.

## 2. The 9 blocks in working depth

### Block 1 — Problem

List the **top 1-3 problems** the customer faces. Plus:

**Existing alternatives** — how do customers solve this today?

The existing-alternatives sub-block is critical. Customers always have a
workaround (spreadsheet, manual process, competing product, doing nothing).
Naming it tells you:
- The bar your product must clear
- The price they currently pay (in time, money, frustration)
- Your switching cost narrative

Common errors:
- Listing 10 problems (lose focus)
- "Inefficiency" as a problem (not specific)
- No existing alternative (always exists; find it)

### Block 2 — Customer Segments

Especially: **Early Adopters**. The first paying customers — those who
are most actively seeking what you're building.

Early Adopters:
- Know they have the problem
- Have tried alternatives
- Are willing to pay for a better solution
- Will give honest feedback
- May not be your eventual mainstream segment

Common errors:
- "Tech-forward enterprises" — too vague
- "Anyone who needs X" — no specificity
- Confusing user with buyer (especially B2B)

### Block 3 — Unique Value Proposition (UVP)

Single, clear, compelling message that turns visitors into prospects.

Template: "**[High-level outcome]** for **[early adopter segment]** that
**[differentiator from existing alternatives]**."

Example: "Cut SOC 2 audit prep from 6 months to 6 weeks for mid-market
SaaS — by automating 80% of the evidence collection your auditor asks for."

Common errors:
- "World-class X for everyone" (vague)
- Feature list (not value)
- "Better, faster, cheaper" (everyone's pitch)
- > 1 sentence (becomes a paragraph; nobody reads)

### Block 4 — Solution

Top 1-3 features (NOT 10). Each feature maps to a Problem.

A Solution block with 8 features signals founder lost discipline.
Bring it back to "what does this product *do* in 3 bullets?"

Common errors:
- Feature list of everything possible
- Solution that doesn't map to the listed Problems
- Solution before Problem (solution-in-search-of-problem)

### Block 5 — Channels

Path(s) to reach customers. For startups, focus on channels you can
test cheaply in weeks:

- Founder-led sales (calls, conferences)
- Content (one piece per week, measured)
- Community engagement (Slack, Discord, subreddit)
- Direct outreach (cold email, LinkedIn)
- Paid acquisition (small budget, test)

Common errors:
- "Online marketing" (not specific)
- All channels listed = no channel tested
- Listing channels you can't yet afford
- No CAC estimate

### Block 6 — Revenue Streams

Revenue model + LTV + forecast.

For startups, capture:
- **Pricing model:** freemium / subscription / usage / transaction
- **Price point:** specific number (even if hypothesis)
- **LTV estimate:** months × MRR × gross margin (rough)
- **First $X thousand revenue plan:** how do you get to $10K MRR?

Common errors:
- "TBD" (decide; can iterate)
- LTV stacked on LTV stacked on LTV (every assumption inflated)
- Free forever model with no path to monetize

### Block 7 — Cost Structure

CAC + distribution + people + hosting.

For a startup, distinguish:
- **Fixed monthly burn** (people, tools, infra at zero customers)
- **Variable per-customer cost** (CAC, support, infra)
- **Capital-intensive items** (if any)

Common errors:
- Forgetting CAC (the biggest cost line)
- Generic "salaries, rent, marketing"
- Ignoring infrastructure scaling cost

### Block 8 — Key Metrics

The key activities you measure (and a single one to optimize for).

Pirate metrics (AAARRR):
- Acquisition
- Activation
- Retention
- Referral
- Revenue
- (or shift to Engagement first)

Pick **one** as North Star. Others are inputs.

Common errors:
- 12 metrics tracked, nothing optimized
- "Revenue" as North Star (lagging)
- Metrics that don't tie to value delivered

### Block 9 — Unfair Advantage

Something that cannot be easily copied or bought.

Maurya: "A real unfair advantage is something that cannot be easily
copied or bought."

What counts:
- Insider information (proprietary data, deep customer relationships)
- Authority / dream team (recognized expertise)
- Personal endorsements / network
- Existing customers / community
- Network effects already started
- Patented IP

What doesn't count:
- "We move fast"
- "Better UX"
- "Our team is great"
- "First mover" (in most cases)
- "AI" / "ML" (commodity now)
- "Cheaper" (race to zero)

Most startups have NO unfair advantage at start. The honest play:
mark "none yet — will build via [X]" and explicitly plan to acquire one.

## 3. The early-adopter test

A useful test for whether Lean Canvas is grounded:

**Name 5 specific early adopters by name + their existing alternative.**

If you can't, you're not at problem-solution fit. The canvas is hypothesis,
not validation.

## 4. Assumption register

Every Lean Canvas implies assumptions. List the riskiest:

| Block | Assumption | Risk | Evidence | Test |
|-------|------------|------|----------|------|
| Problem | Segment X has this problem with frequency Y | high | low | 10 customer interviews |
| Channel | Cold outreach converts at 2% | high | none | 100-email test |
| Revenue | Segment X will pay $50/mo | high | low | landing page + waitlist |

Test the highest risk + lowest evidence first.

## 5. When to update the canvas

- Every quarter (refresh)
- After major experiments (incorporate learnings)
- After pivots (new canvas; archive old)
- Before fundraising (investor-facing version)
- Before strategic offsite (working version)

A static Lean Canvas signals lack of learning.

## 6. The 1-page rule

Lean Canvas must fit on 1 page. If you can't:
- Your Solution has too many features
- Your Problem list is too long
- Your UVP isn't sharp

Force the discipline. Excess prose hides muddled thinking.
