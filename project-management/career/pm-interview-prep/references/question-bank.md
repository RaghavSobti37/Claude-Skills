# PM Interview Question Bank

A curated bank organized by round type and difficulty. Use the level column to pick questions matched to the role you are interviewing for.

Levels: **APM** (associate / entry-level), **PM** (mid-level IC), **SR** (senior PM), **GPM** (group PM, leads PMs).

---

## 1. Product Sense (Design)

### Easy / APM-friendly

| Q | Level | Focus |
|---|-------|-------|
| Design an alarm clock for a blind person. | APM | Empathy + constraints |
| Design a new feature for your favorite app to drive engagement. | APM | Creativity within familiar context |
| If you could redesign the TV remote, what would you change? | APM | Pain identification |
| Design a product that helps people drink more water. | APM | Habit formation |

### Medium / PM-level

| Q | Level | Focus |
|---|-------|-------|
| Design a product for elderly people to stay connected with family. | PM | Segment empathy + tech literacy |
| Redesign the airport security experience. | PM | System-level redesign |
| Design a Spotify feature for people who use it while working. | PM | Contextual segmentation |
| Design a tool for new parents in their first 3 months. | PM | Emotional state design |
| Design a feature for LinkedIn that helps people get their next job. | PM | Outcome-driven design |
| If Uber were starting today, what would you build differently? | PM | Strategic redesign |

### Hard / Senior+

| Q | Level | Focus |
|---|-------|-------|
| Design a product for the next 1 billion internet users. | SR | Emerging market constraints |
| Design a regulatory-compliant product for a US-based crypto exchange. | SR | Constraint dominance |
| Design a platform feature that 3 different teams could build on. | SR | Platform thinking |
| Reimagine the public library for 2030. | GPM | Institution-scale design |
| Design a product that reduces social isolation for remote workers. | GPM | Org-of-products thinking |

## 4. Behavioral

Use the 12-story prompt list. The most common variants asked at every level:

| Theme | Common Question Variant |
|-------|-------------------------|
| Influence without authority | "Tell me about a time you led without being the boss." |
| Disagree with manager | "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager. What did you do?" |
| Kill a project | "Tell me about a project you killed. How did you decide?" |
| Major mistake | "What's the biggest mistake you've made? What did you learn?" |
| Turnaround | "Tell me about a struggling project you turned around." |
| Say no | "Tell me about a time you pushed back on a stakeholder." |
| Missed deadline | "Tell me about a project that slipped. What happened?" |
| Biggest impact | "What's the project you're most proud of? Why?" |
| Difficult partner | "Tell me about a time you worked with someone difficult." |
| Mentor / develop (SR+) | "Tell me about someone you developed. What did you do?" |
| Process / system (SR+) | "Tell me about a process you created or evolved." |
| Set strategy (GPM+) | "Tell me about a strategy you set that the org adopted." |

### Less-common but high-signal

- "Tell me about a time your data said one thing and your gut said another. What did you do?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to deliver bad news to your team."
- "Tell me about a customer interaction that changed how you think about the product."
- "Tell me about a time you had to choose between two equally good options."

## How to use this bank

1. Pick 5-10 questions per round type at your target level.
2. Time yourself answering each in the round's typical length.
3. Record yourself. Listen back for filler words, framework-reciting, and skipped steps.
4. Rotate questions weekly so you do not memorize answers.
5. For the final 2 weeks before a loop, focus exclusively on company-specific products and questions sourced from `levels.fyi` or `glassdoor` for that company.
