## Opening line (rehearse this verbatim)

> "______________________________________________________________"

A strong opening line hooks the interviewer and sets the frame. Examples:

- "About 18 months into my role at [company], I led the decision to kill a project we had been building for 6 months."
- "The most ambitious project I've led was a payments platform rebuild that touched 4 teams and shipped on time."
- "I'll share a story where I disagreed with my manager about whether to launch."

## Closing line (rehearse this too)

> "______________________________________________________________"

A strong closing line names the learning and transfers it to the interviewer.

- "The big lesson for me was that bringing a counter-plan beats bringing a counter-argument. I've used that pattern many times since."
- "If I had to do it again, I would push for the data sooner -- I was three weeks late to that conversation."
- "What I take from this is that the best PM decisions are reversible decisions made faster, not perfect decisions made slowly."
