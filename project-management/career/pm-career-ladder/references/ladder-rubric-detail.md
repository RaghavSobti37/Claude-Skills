# PM Ladder Rubric -- Expanded Detail

For each of the five dimensions, this document expands the rubric across all levels with:

- **Looks like** -- Specific observable behaviors
- **Anti-patterns** -- Common confusions about the bar at that level
- **Promotion signal** -- What unlocks the next level

---

## Dimension 1: Product Sense

### APM
- **Looks like:** Translates manager-defined problems into shippable features; uses existing customer research; sense-checks designs against the brief.
- **Anti-pattern:** "I generated 50 ideas" — quantity is not the bar at APM. Ship a tight 2-3.
- **Promotion signal:** Independently re-scopes a feature based on customer feedback in the middle of a build.

### PM
- **Looks like:** Frames problems for the area; runs discovery interviews; maintains a prioritized opportunity backlog; sense-checks competitor moves.
- **Anti-pattern:** Frames every problem as a feature spec. PMs at this level should frame problems as customer needs first.
- **Promotion signal:** Identifies an opportunity that wasn't on the roadmap and successfully argues for resourcing it.

### Senior PM
- **Looks like:** Builds a multi-quarter opportunity space for the area; runs continuous discovery (weekly customer touch); designs the metric tree; spots adjacent opportunities other areas are missing.
- **Anti-pattern:** Confuses "I have opinions" with "I have customer evidence". Sr PM bar is customer-grounded opinions.
- **Promotion signal:** Has shaped strategy for an adjacent area without authority.

### Group PM
- **Looks like:** Sets strategic posture across multiple sub-areas; identifies platform opportunities; chooses which markets/segments to over- or under-invest in.
- **Anti-pattern:** Plays each team's product sense back to them. GPM bar is original strategic synthesis across teams.
- **Promotion signal:** Has identified a portfolio-level bet that the org embraced.

### Director
- **Looks like:** Allocates capital across a portfolio; sets vision for the product line; identifies "moat" investments.
- **Anti-pattern:** Becomes a glorified GPM. Director bar is portfolio thinking, not team-level steering.
- **Promotion signal:** Owns a measurable business outcome (revenue, retention, share) for the portfolio.

### VP / CPO
- **Looks like:** Sets company product vision and 3-5 year strategy; aligns to company strategy; partners with the CEO on bets.
- **Anti-pattern:** Treats the role as "Director++" instead of company-level product leadership.

## Dimension 2: Execution

### APM
- **Looks like:** Drives one feature end-to-end; runs standups and demos; tracks bugs; communicates timelines.
- **Anti-pattern:** Becomes a project tracker. PM at every level adds judgment, not just status.
- **Promotion signal:** Ships >=2 features with measurable impact and no manager intervention.

### PM
- **Looks like:** Owns the area's delivery; runs all team rituals; manages risk and dependencies; communicates timelines proactively.
- **Anti-pattern:** Confuses busyness with execution. Operating cadence is the point, not the calendar.
- **Promotion signal:** Designs a recurring operating cadence that other PMs copy.

### Senior PM
- **Looks like:** Designs the operating system for the team (planning, review, retro); unblocks systemic delivery issues; manages risk at the program level.
- **Anti-pattern:** Spends 80% of time in delivery rituals. Sr PM should redirect 30%+ of time from delivery into discovery and strategy.
- **Promotion signal:** Has redesigned a delivery process that improved a metric (cycle time, predictability).

### Group PM
- **Looks like:** Coordinates execution across multiple teams; manages cross-team dependencies; ensures delivery against the product-line strategy.
- **Anti-pattern:** Becomes the dependency-tracker for the org. GPM should design the operating model so dependencies surface earlier, not chase them.
- **Promotion signal:** Has built an operating cadence across teams that survives a quarter without GPM intervention.

### Director
- **Looks like:** Sets the portfolio operating cadence; ensures predictable delivery across teams; designs the metrics by which delivery is judged.

### VP / CPO
- **Looks like:** Owns the org-wide operating model; builds capabilities (hiring, training, tooling); ensures the system runs without daily VP involvement.

## Dimension 3: Leadership

### APM
- **Looks like:** Builds trust with engineers and designers in the trio; receives feedback well; owns mistakes.
- **Anti-pattern:** Apologetic or deferential. APM bar is "valued thought partner", not "junior staffer".
- **Promotion signal:** A peer engineer or designer requests to work with the APM again.

### PM
- **Looks like:** Earns trust as the product lead; resolves cross-functional disagreements; mentors APMs informally; says no with reasoning.
- **Anti-pattern:** Treats leadership as a title. PM bar is "people follow your reasoning", not "people follow you".
- **Promotion signal:** Has mentored a peer or junior through a hard problem with measurable outcome.

### Senior PM
- **Looks like:** Mentors junior PMs formally or informally; influences across functions; is sought out for advice by peers; develops successors.
- **Anti-pattern:** Mentors only juniors. Sr PM bar includes lateral and upward influence.
- **Promotion signal:** Has developed a PM who promoted; has influenced an exec decision.

### Group PM
- **Looks like:** Leads a small group of PMs (formally or informally); sets bar for craft; develops the next layer; coaches managers.
- **Anti-pattern:** "Player-coach" who never actually coaches. GPM bar is real development of others.
- **Promotion signal:** Has a bench of promotable PMs; is requested for skip-level conversations.

### Director
- **Looks like:** Manages PM managers; builds the org structure; develops the next layer of leadership; sets hiring bar.

### VP / CPO
- **Looks like:** Builds the product org; develops VP and Director successors; partners with CEO on org-design decisions.

## Dimension 4: Strategy

### APM
- **Looks like:** Understands the team's strategy and can articulate it; translates strategy into feature trade-offs.
- **Anti-pattern:** Cannot articulate why the team is doing X vs. Y. APM bar is "knows the why".

### PM
- **Looks like:** Owns the area's roadmap with rationale; can defend trade-offs in writing; connects roadmap to business outcomes.
- **Anti-pattern:** Roadmap is a list of features without rationale. PM bar is "every line item has a why".
- **Promotion signal:** Successfully argues for or against a strategic move using data and reasoning.

### Senior PM
- **Looks like:** Connects area strategy to company strategy; defends trade-offs to leadership; identifies strategic risks proactively.
- **Anti-pattern:** Strategy is "I read Lenny's newsletter". Sr PM bar is "I have an original POV grounded in evidence".
- **Promotion signal:** Has shaped the company's strategy in their area.

### Group PM
- **Looks like:** Sets multi-quarter strategy for the product line; aligns the org; manages portfolio trade-offs.
- **Anti-pattern:** Strategy as a planning artifact, not a living system. GPM bar is "strategy gets reviewed and updated quarterly with data".
- **Promotion signal:** Has run a portfolio review that changed allocation.

### Director
- **Looks like:** Owns portfolio strategy; allocates capital; partners with finance on business case.

### VP / CPO
- **Looks like:** Owns company product strategy; partners with CEO; board-facing.

---

## Dimension 5: Communication

### APM
- **Looks like:** Clear written updates to the team; runs effective standups and demos.
- **Anti-pattern:** Updates are status without context. APM bar is "the reader knows what to do next".

### PM
- **Looks like:** Crisp PRDs that pass the 10-second exec scan; effective stakeholder presentations; written status updates.
- **Anti-pattern:** Documents are long and uncentered. PM bar is "leads with the decision, not the journey".
- **Promotion signal:** A document of theirs gets reused by other PMs or referenced upward.

### Senior PM
- **Looks like:** Effective exec readouts; persuasive writing that drives decisions; presents at company-wide forums.
- **Anti-pattern:** Communication is reactive (reply when asked). Sr PM bar is "shapes the narrative proactively".
- **Promotion signal:** Has authored a doc that influenced an exec decision.

### Group PM
- **Looks like:** Sets the narrative for the product line; aligns multi-team stakeholders; speaks for the product at company forums.
- **Anti-pattern:** Communication is internal only. GPM bar includes external-facing (customers, partners, candidates).

### Director
- **Looks like:** Sets exec-level narrative; represents product externally; high-stakes written communication.

### VP / CPO
- **Looks like:** Speaks for the company on product; analyst- and customer-facing; board-facing.

---

## Cross-cutting promotion signals

Regardless of dimension, three signals predict promotion:

1. **Operating at the next level for >=2 quarters consistently.** Promotion confirms what is already true.
2. **Bench effects.** You have developed someone who can backfill you, freeing leadership to promote.
3. **Stakeholder support.** Skip-levels, cross-functional leads, and peers actively advocate for the promotion in calibration.

The packet is the artifact, but the substance is the operating pattern.
