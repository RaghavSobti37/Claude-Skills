# Example: Maria Chen — APM-to-PM Promotion Packet

> Real-world scenario showing how to apply this skill end-to-end.

## Context

Maria Chen has been an APM at Wayfinder Analytics (Series-B B2B analytics, ~180 employees) for 16 months. She joined out of a top APM rotational program with no prior PM experience. Her manager, Daniel, believes she is ready for the PM level and has asked her to assemble a promo packet for the next promotion cycle, which closes 2026-06-15.

Wayfinder runs a calibrated promo process: candidates submit a packet, the candidate's manager presents at calibration, and a cross-functional panel (3 directors + 1 VP) votes. Last cycle, 4 of 7 APM-to-PM submissions were promoted. The rubric in this skill maps cleanly to Wayfinder's published ladder, so Maria is using it to do a structured self-assessment and to build the packet.

## Inputs

- Maria's level: APM, 16 months in role
- Target level: PM
- Manager: Daniel (Director, Activation product line)
- Wayfinder rubric: APM, PM, Sr PM, GPM, Director (mirrors the skill ladder)
- Recent work: led the redesign of the trial-signup flow; ran a Q1 experiment that lifted day-7 activation by 11%; mentored a brand-new APM (Jordan) for the last 3 months
- Constraint: packet must fit a 3-page template + 2 references; 30-min calibration slot

## Applying the skill

1. **Self-assessed against all five dimensions.** Maria used the rubric to score herself APM/PM/Sr-PM bar per dimension. Honest scoring matters — overclaiming gets caught at calibration.
2. **Gap analysis.** Identified which PM-level behaviors she is already demonstrating versus aspirational. Two dimensions (Product Sense, Execution) are solid PM; two (Communication, Leadership) are PM-equivalent for her area but not yet cross-functional; one (Strategy) is the gap.
3. **Built the packet around evidence, not narrative.** Each claim is backed by an artifact link (PRD, post-mortem, experiment readout, peer feedback).
4. **Wrote the 6-month growth plan as a hedge.** Even though the packet is a promo bid, Maria attached a growth plan that runs whether or not she is promoted — signaling self-direction.
5. **Built a 30-day strategy plan to address the gap.** The strategy dimension was her weakest score. Rather than hide it, she included a written 30-day plan to close the gap and asked Daniel to validate.

## Why this works

- **Honest self-assessment.** Maria scored herself APM in Strategy. A weaker packet would have inflated everything to PM. Calibration panels detect inflation immediately — once they catch one over-claim, they re-read the whole packet skeptically.
- **Evidence per claim.** Every claim is anchored to a linked artifact, not a narrative. The panel can verify in 90 seconds.
- **Faced the gap.** Strategy was the weakest dimension, and Maria led with it on page 3 rather than burying it. She paired it with a 30-day plan that Daniel pre-approved. This converts a weakness into a proof-of-trajectory.
- **References named specific behaviors, not adjectives.** The Tomas quote is "she authored a multi-team OKR I borrowed verbatim" — not "she is great." Specifics survive panel pushback.
- **Mentorship case strengthened the packet.** Mentoring Jordan is technically a Sr PM behavior; Maria did it as an APM. This is the kind of "operating above level" signal that panels promote on, more than any single feature ship.
- **Growth plan runs either way.** Attaching a 6-month plan that does not depend on the promo decision signals self-direction and removes the appearance of entitlement.
